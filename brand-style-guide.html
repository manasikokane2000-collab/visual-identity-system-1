Logo usage rules
Do — Light backgrounds

Use the full-color logo on cream (#F8F5F0), white, or light neutral backgrounds. The orange accent must have sufficient contrast against the background.

Don't — Busy backgrounds

Never place the logo on photographs, patterned backgrounds, or surfaces where the mark disappears. Use a white or dark badge container instead.

Do — Dark backgrounds

On dark surfaces, use the white version of the logotype with the orange accent mark. Minimum size: 24px height for digital, 10mm for print.

Don't — Distort or recolor

Never stretch, rotate, apply drop shadows, outline, or change the logo colors to anything outside the official brand palette.

Do — Clear space

Maintain a clear space equal to the height of the logo mark on all sides. No other elements, text, or imagery should enter this zone.

Don't — Low resolution

Never use raster (JPG/PNG) logo files at sizes where they appear blurry. Always use SVG for digital and vector PDF/EPS for print.

04 — Typography Rules

Usage Guidelines

Rules for applying the type system consistently across all brand touchpoints.

✓ Do
Bold headline here
Body text follows the heading with adequate size contrast. Minimum 1.6× size difference between heading and body for clear hierarchy.
Label tag · eyebrow text
✗ Don't
Heading too similar
When heading and body are nearly the same size there is no visual hierarchy. Readers cannot distinguish what matters most on the page.
label with no treatment
✓ Do — Line length
Keep body text between 55–75 characters per line. This paragraph is the right width — comfortable to read without eye fatigue across multiple sentences.
✗ Don't — Full width body
Stretching body text to full column width makes reading difficult. The eye has to travel too far across the line and loses its place when returning to the start of the next line.
